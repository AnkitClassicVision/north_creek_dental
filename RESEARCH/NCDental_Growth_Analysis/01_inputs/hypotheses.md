# Research Hypotheses: North Creek Dental Care

## Market Hypotheses

### H-M1: All-on-4 Market Growth
**Statement**: The All-on-4/full mouth implant market in Chicago Southland is growing at 8-12% annually due to aging demographics and increased awareness.
**Prior Probability**: 0.75
**Prior Reasoning**: National trends show strong implant market growth; aging baby boomer population in suburbs
**Confirming Evidence**:
- Market research showing implant growth rates
- Demographic data on 55+ population in service area
- Google Trends data for "dental implants" in Chicago area
**Disconfirming Evidence**:
- Market saturation data
- Economic downturn impact on elective procedures
- Insurance coverage limitations
**Test Queries**:
- "dental implant market growth 2024 2025"
- "All-on-4 market size United States"
- "Chicago suburbs aging population demographics"
**Status**: testing

### H-M2: Primary Decision Factor
**Statement**: The primary decision factor for All-on-4 patients is TRUST/EXPERTISE, not price, due to the high-stakes nature of the procedure.
**Prior Probability**: 0.70
**Prior Reasoning**: High-ticket irreversible procedures favor expertise over cost; patient reviews emphasize doctor skill
**Confirming Evidence**:
- Patient survey data on decision factors
- Review analysis showing expertise mentions
- Conversion rate data by messaging type
**Disconfirming Evidence**:
- High price sensitivity in consultations
- Competitor success with low-price positioning
- Financing as primary conversion driver
**Test Queries**:
- "dental implant patient decision factors survey"
- "All-on-4 patient reviews what matters"
- "high ticket dental procedure conversion factors"
**Status**: testing

## Competitive Hypotheses

### H-C1: Competitive Advantage
**Statement**: NCDental's primary competitive advantage is having in-house specialists (prosthodontist + periodontist) eliminating referral friction.
**Prior Probability**: 0.80
**Prior Reasoning**: Most general practices refer out for complex implant work; one-stop-shop is rare
**Confirming Evidence**:
- Competitor analysis showing referral models
- Patient reviews mentioning convenience
- Market research on patient preferences
**Disconfirming Evidence**:
- Competitors with similar in-house model
- Patients preferring specialist-only practices
- Brand/reputation outweighing convenience
**Test Queries**:
- "dental implant practices Chicago south suburbs"
- "All-on-4 providers Tinley Park Orland Park"
- "in-house dental implant specialists advantage"
**Status**: testing

### H-C2: Competitive Threat
**Statement**: The biggest competitive threat is DSO-backed practices (Aspen, ClearChoice) with heavy advertising budgets and aggressive pricing.
**Prior Probability**: 0.70
**Prior Reasoning**: DSOs dominate implant advertising; ClearChoice specifically targets All-on-4 market
**Confirming Evidence**:
- DSO presence in market
- Advertising spend analysis
- Patient flow to DSO practices
**Disconfirming Evidence**:
- Local practice loyalty
- DSO reputation issues
- Geographic distance from DSO locations
**Test Queries**:
- "ClearChoice Chicago locations"
- "Aspen Dental implants Chicago"
- "DSO dental market share Illinois"
**Status**: testing

### H-C3: Differentiation Opportunity
**Statement**: A differentiation opportunity exists in emphasizing clinical credentials (board-certified periodontist, 40+ years experience) vs. DSO "volume" model.
**Prior Probability**: 0.75
**Prior Reasoning**: DSOs face criticism for turnover and quality; NCDental has exceptional credentials
**Confirming Evidence**:
- DSO negative reviews patterns
- Patient preference for experienced doctors
- Successful differentiation examples
**Disconfirming Evidence**:
- Credentials not resonating with target market
- Price dominating decision
- DSO improving quality perception
**Test Queries**:
- "ClearChoice reviews complaints"
- "dental implant patient concerns"
- "choosing implant dentist credentials vs price"
**Status**: testing

## Growth Hypotheses

### H-G1: Highest-ROI Channel
**Statement**: The highest-ROI marketing channel for All-on-4 is Google Ads targeting high-intent keywords, followed by retargeting.
**Prior Probability**: 0.70
**Prior Reasoning**: High-ticket considered purchase; search intent is strong signal; long research cycle favors retargeting
**Confirming Evidence**:
- Dental marketing ROI benchmarks
- Google Ads case studies for implants
- Attribution data from similar practices
**Disconfirming Evidence**:
- Social media outperforming search
- Referral/word-of-mouth dominance
- TV/radio effectiveness in market
**Test Queries**:
- "dental implant marketing ROI by channel"
- "All-on-4 patient acquisition cost"
- "Google Ads dental implant conversion rate"
**Status**: testing

### H-G2: Growth Barrier
**Statement**: The primary barrier to growth is consultation-to-procedure conversion rate, not lead volume.
**Prior Probability**: 0.65
**Prior Reasoning**: High-ticket procedures have long sales cycles; many consultations don't convert
**Confirming Evidence**:
- Industry consultation conversion benchmarks
- Patient objection analysis
- Follow-up process effectiveness data
**Disconfirming Evidence**:
- Lead volume as limiting factor
- High existing conversion rates
- Market awareness as barrier
**Test Queries**:
- "dental implant consultation conversion rate benchmark"
- "All-on-4 sales cycle length"
- "dental implant patient objections"
**Status**: testing

### H-G3: Financing Impact
**Statement**: Offering prominent financing options (CareCredit, in-house plans) can increase conversion by 25-40% for procedures over $10k.
**Prior Probability**: 0.75
**Prior Reasoning**: National data shows financing lifts conversion significantly for high-ticket dental
**Confirming Evidence**:
- CareCredit conversion lift data
- Dental financing case studies
- Patient survey on payment barriers
**Disconfirming Evidence**:
- Target demographic doesn't need financing
- Financing friction offsetting benefits
- Cash/insurance coverage adequate
**Test Queries**:
- "dental financing conversion rate increase"
- "CareCredit dental implant case study"
- "patient financing high ticket dental procedures"
**Status**: testing
